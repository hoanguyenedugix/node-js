Activity A: Listen. Then act it out.
15
Stage aims
  • To help students practice “Have you got…?” and sports-related vocabulary by listening to a story and acting it out. This stage reinforces comprehension and speaking skills in an engaging and interactive way.
Context
Stage 1: Setting the Scene
  • Announce: “It’s a sunny day, and the school is buzzing with excitement—because it’s Sports Day!”
  • Introduce the main characters: “Amy, Nick, and Tim are in the park.”
  • Pause and reveal the problem: “Uh-oh… they can’t find everything they need to play!”
  • Build anticipation: “Let’s listen to their story and find out what happens!”
Heads up
Focus
  • Draw Ss attention to the CPT.
  • Say, let’s read.
Presentation
Skimming
  • Where are the children? (in the park)
Scanning
  • Which children can you see? (Nick, Amy, Tim)
Comprehension
  • Look at picture 1. What game does Nick want to play? (cricket)
  • Look at picture 2. What does Nick have? (he has a bat)
  • Look at picture 3. Does Amy have a glove? (no, she doesn’t)
  • Look at picture 4. What does Tim have? (he has a soccer ball)
  • Look at picture 5. Can the children play cricket? (no, they can’t)
  • Look at picture 6. What game do they play? (they play tag)
Heads down
Instruction
  • Ask Ss to take books out of their bags.
  • Ask Ss to open books. Countdown from 5 to keep time.
  • Use gestures. Say ‘Listen, read and point’.
ICQs
  • Ask ‘Are we going to listen nicely?’ (yes)
  • Ask ‘Are we going to point or write? (point)
Activity and monitor
  • Play the audio. Pause if necessary. Play again.
  • Monitor. Provide support/stretch if needed.
Feedback
  • Feedback on errors if needed.
  • Review answers, correct mistakes together.

Activity B: Look and match
20
Stage aims
  • To develop students’ reading comprehension and vocabulary recognition by matching characters (Amy, Nick, Tim) to what they have, using information from the story. This stage reinforces understanding of “has got” in context before moving on to speaking or writing activities.
Context
Stage 2: Identifying What They Have
  • React enthusiastically: “You all acted out the story so well! It felt like we were right there with Amy, Nick, and Tim.”
  • Reflect: “Now, let’s think carefully—what do they already have to play cricket?”
  • Act puzzled: “Hmm… Was it a yo-yo? A bat? A football?”
  • Say: “Let’s look closely at their things and figure it out together!”
Heads up
Presentation
  • Ask Ss who they can see.
  • Ask Ss to read the sentences.
  • Ask Ss where they can find the answers (story page).
Heads down
Instruction
  • Ask Ss to get pencils. Countdown from 5 to keep time.
  • Use gestures. Say ‘Look and match’.
ICQs
  • Ask: Are you going to speak or draw lines? (draw lines)
  • Ask: Are you going to work alone or together? (alone)
Activity and monitor
  • Monitor. Provide support/stretch if needed.
Feedback
  • Feedback on errors if needed.
  • Have the Ss pair-share.
  • Review answers, correct mistakes together.

Activity Book: Activity A
15
Stage aims
  • To develop students’ reading comprehension and sequencing skills by reading a new story in the activity book and numbering events in the correct order. This stage helps students understand story structure and reinforces the use of “has got” in context.
Homework correction
  • Have Ss open to the previous lesson’s homework.
  • Have the Ss pair-share.
  • Review answers, correct mistakes together.
Context
Stage 3: Checking for Missing Items
  • Praise their effort: “Great detective work! Now we know what Amy, Nick, and Tim have.”
  • Introduce the next challenge: “But they still need something to complete their game.”
  • Say: “Let’s check the story again to make sure we remember everything correctly.”
  • Build curiosity: “This time, we’ll go back through the story and pay close attention!”
Heads down
Instruction
  • Ask Ss to take books and pencils out of their bags.
  • Ask Ss to open books. Countdown from 5 to keep time.
  • Use gestures. Say Read and number.
ICQs
  • Ask ‘Do we write neatly?’ (yes)
  • Ask ‘Do we help each other? (yes)
During
  • Monitor. Provide support/stretch if needed.
After
  • Have the Ss pair-share.
  • Review answers, correct mistakes together.
  • Mark the Ss’ books with a pen.
Homework setting
  • Complete question 1 of activity B with the Ss.
  • Complete question 1 of activity C with the Ss.
  • Check errors. Check understanding.
  • Have the Ss out their books and pencils away.
Reward moment
  • Centre yourself, settle the class.
  • Check behaviour expectations.
  • Reward/remind and award points.
Activity C: Make Your Own Story
25
Stage aims
  • To encourage creativity and language production by having students create their own version of the story using stickers. This stage reinforces key vocabulary and sentence structures while allowing students to personalize their learning.
Context

Tongue Twisters
20
Stage aims
  • To develop students’ pronunciation and fluency through fun tongue twisters, focusing on clear articulation of sounds like /l/ and /r/. This stage helps improve speech clarity and confidence in speaking.
Context
Stage 5: Wrapping Up & Reflection
  • Celebrate: “Wow, that was fantastic! Amy, Nick, and Tim are so happy they can finally play cricket—and it’s all thanks to you!”
  • Reflect: “Let’s take a moment to think about everything we did today.”
  • Ask: “What was your favorite part of the story?”
  • Now let’s have some fun with tongue twisters!
Heads up
Focus
  • Draw Ss attention to the CPT.
  • Say, let’s read.
Presentation
Meaning
  • Ask questions about the 2 pictures, drawing attention to the lion’s legs and rabbits car colour.
Form
  • Focus on the 4 ls and 4 rs. Show the shape of the mouth.
Pronunciation
  • Drill sentences (class, groups, individual).
CCQs
  • Mime: ‘l’, Ss guess (They say ‘l’)
  • Mime: ‘r’, Ss guess (They say ‘r’)
  • Mime a few of the ‘l’ and ‘r’ words, Ss guess.
Heads down
Instruction
  • Use gestures. Say: listen and say.
ICQs
  • Ask ‘Are you going to shout? (no)
  • Ask ‘Are you going to read and say? (yes)
Activity and monitor
  • Monitor. Provide support/stretch if needed.
Feedback
  • Feedback on errors if needed.
  • Review answers, correct mistakes together.
